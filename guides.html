Types of Crypto Wallets

1. Hot Wallets (Software Wallets)

Best for: Frequent deposits and withdrawals at crypto casinos.

Examples: MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Exodus, Electrum

Pros: Fast, convenient, easy to use, free

Cons: Less secure than cold wallets (connected to internet)

2. Cold Wallets (Hardware Wallets)

Best for: Storing large amounts of crypto long-term.

Examples: Ledger Nano X, Trezor Model T

Pros: Maximum security (offline storage), protects against hacks

Cons: Costs $50-200, less convenient for frequent transactions

3. Exchange Wallets

Best for: Buying crypto and transferring to gambling wallets.

Examples: Coinbase, Binance, Kraken

Pros: Easy to buy crypto with fiat, user-friendly

Cons: Not your keys = not your crypto
